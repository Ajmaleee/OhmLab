Resistor History & Fun Facts

The word “resistor” became common as electronics matured, but the idea of restricting current goes back to early electrical experiments. Fun fact: many classic radios used large, colorless resistors marked with printed values, and technicians relied on lookup charts to decode them. Today’s color bands made the process faster and reduced errors. Understanding those roots reminds us why precise resistance values remain critical in safe circuit design.
